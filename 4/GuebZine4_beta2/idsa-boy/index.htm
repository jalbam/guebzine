  Como bien sabreis, la IDSA, o Interactive Digital Software Association continua cerrando
inofensivas páginas de emulación, y nosotros, la  gente a la que nos gustan los juegos antiguos,
 lo hemos intentado todo para pararlos. Hemos intentado hablar con ellos, hemos intentado
implicar a los medios de comunicación. Nada de esto parece funcionar,  y  la  IDSA continua
 cerrando página tras página  de inofensivas y anticuadas ROMs.
 
¿Qué podemos hacer entonces? ¿Tenemos alguna oportunidad contra ellos? Puede ser.
Ya que la IDSA sólo está interesada en el dinero, se lo quitaremos. Estoy seguro de que
la mayoría de vosotros compra juegos modernos, pues si todos paramos de comprar
juegos , la IDSA se dará cuenta de lo que se siente cuando se pierde dinero, y podriamos
negociar con ellos para que nos dejen jugar en paz con los juegos antiguos.
 
 
La carta de debajo (está en Inglés, evidentemente) anuncia el boicot a todas las compañias
afiliadas a la IDSA. Por favor, copiala, pégala y pon tu nombre al final. Mándala a la
siguiente dirección de E-mail:   idsa@idsa.com . Luego, por favor, si te importa algo
la emulación, no compres ningún juego nuevo hasta que la IDSA acepte nuestras demandas.
Vale la pena  que pares de comprar juegos nuevos para preservar y jugar a los antiguos.
 
 
 Dear Sir or Madam:
   We, the retrogamer community, are displeased with your effort to shut down the "emulation" scene on the
Internet. We have politely requested numerous times that you stop shutting down websites that contain ROMs for
  outdated games, and we have continued to support your member companies by purchasing modern games
 available for the Sony Playstation, Nintendo 64, PC, and Macintosh. Apparently, we believed that we could stop
 you from making such mindless decisions. Our requests have gone unanswered, and you continue to shut down
  site after site of harmless obsolete game ROMs. Therefore we, the retrogamer community, are announcing a
           boycott of all IDSA-affilated companies, until the terms stated in this letter are met.
 Please allow all ROMs for games 10 years old or older that were designed by your member companies to be
 distributed over the Internet, as public domain software. You have every right to retain the copyrights as long as
you want, however you must allow these games to be distributed freely. Computer software is designed to make
  its money back in 6 months, so 10 years is plenty of time to gain profit on a video game. You may re-release
  classic video game compilations for those who are perhaps less computer-literate. You may also prosecute
 people who try to sell these ROMs for a fee. In addition, you can prosecute anyone or shut down any site which
                         has roms that are less than 10 years old.
   We believe that these terms are very reasonable. When you have met these terms, we will discontinue the
           boycott.
           Sincerely,
            (tu nombre)
  I, (tu nombre), pledge not to purchase any software developed by IDSA members that do not agree with the
                   above conditions.
